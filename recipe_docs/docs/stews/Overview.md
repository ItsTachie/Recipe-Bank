# 📖 overview

> Slow-cooked, deeply comforting, and packed with flavor. Whether it's a rich beef gravy or a warmly spiced Indian curry, these dishes are perfect for cozy nights in and always taste even better the next day.

---
### 💡 General Tips & Ratios

- **Brown the Meat First:** Never skip the sear! Browning your chunks of beef or chicken in hot oil before adding your liquids creates a deep, savory crust (the Maillard reaction) that flavors the entire pot.
- **Deglaze the Pan:** After searing meat or cooking down onions, pour a splash of broth or water into the hot pan and scrape up all those delicious brown bits stuck to the bottom. That is pure flavor!
- **Bloom Your Spices:** For curries, cook your dry spices (like cumin, coriander, garamasala, and turmeric) in hot oil or butter for 30–60 seconds before adding your wet ingredients (like tomatoes or coconut milk). This "blooms" the spices, releasing their essential oils and making the dish infinitely more flavorful.
- **Low and Slow:** Tough cuts of meat (like stewing beef or oxtail) need time to break down the connective tissue. Keep the heat low and let the pot gently simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours. Boiling it rapidly will actually make the meat tough and rubbery.
