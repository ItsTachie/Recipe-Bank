# Basic Sadza

> *The foundational staple of Zimbabwean cuisine. Cooked low and slow until perfectly firm, ready to be paired with greens, meat, or sour milk.*

**Prep time:** `2 mins` | **Cook time:** `35 mins` | **Servings:** `3`

---
## 🛒 Ingredients

**Main**

* [ ] 1 Mug | Mealie meal (plus extra for folding)
* [ ] 4 Cups | Boiling water

---

## 🍳 Instructions

1. **Prep:** Boil about 4 cups of water in a kettle.
2. **Make the Base:** In your pot, mix a little mealie meal with cold water to make a smooth paste. Pour in the boiling water while stirring constantly to make a thin porridge base.
3. **Simmer:** Keep stirring until it begins to simmer (violently). Reduce the heat to medium-low, put the lid on, and let it simmer for about 15 minutes (kukwata).
4. **Fold (Kumona):** Reduce the heat to low. Start adding dry mealie meal a little at a time, folding and combining it into the porridge with a wooden spoon until you reach the desired consistency (like firm mashed potatoes).
5. **Final Steam:** Put the lid back on and let it cook on low for another 10 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste.
6. **Serve:** Mix the sadza one more time. Use a serving spoon dipped in water or lightly brushed with oil to shape it and prevent sticking.

---
## 💡 Notes & Variations

* **Storage:** Once you make a mound, wrap it tightly in cling foil and store it in the fridge. It reheats wonderfully in the microwave.
